<p><p>Air energy water heaters, commonly known as heat pump water heaters, operate on two primary cycles: the fluorine cycle and the water cycle. The fluorine cycle uses refrigerants, typically fluorinated gases, to absorb ambient air heat and transfer it to water, achieving efficient heating with lower energy consumption. In contrast, the water cycle primarily uses water as a heat transfer medium, leveraging natural sources like groundwater or ambient moisture. Both systems aim to enhance energy efficiency and reduce greenhouse gas emissions in water heating.</p></p>
