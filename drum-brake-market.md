<p><p>Drum brakes are a type of braking system commonly used in vehicles, characterized by a cylindrical drum that rotates with the wheel. <p><ul><li>Leading Trailing Shoe Brake</li><li>Dual Two Trailing Shoe Brake</li><li>Dual Two Leading Shoe Brake</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The drum brake market features three primary types: leading-trailing shoe brakes, dual two-trailing shoe brakes, and dual two-leading shoe brakes. Leading-trailing shoe brakes utilize a single leading and trailing shoe configuration, offering effective braking performance for various vehicles. In contrast, dual two-trailing shoe brakes deploy two trailing shoes for enhanced stopping power, ideal for heavy vehicles. Dual two-leading shoe brakes, featuring two leading shoes, provide superior braking efficiency, commonly used in applications requiring high braking torque. Each design caters to specific performance needs.</p></p>
