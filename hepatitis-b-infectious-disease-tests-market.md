<p><p>The Hepatitis B infectious disease tests market comprises three primary types: antibody tests, antigen tests, and nucleic acid tests (NAT). Antibody tests detect the presence of antibodies produced in response to the virus, indicating past or ongoing infection. Antigen tests identify viral proteins, signaling an active infection. Nucleic acid testing detects the virus's genetic material, providing precise information about viral load and infection status. Together, these tests facilitate accurate diagnosis, monitoring, and management of Hepatitis B infections.</p></p>
