<p><p>The ophthalmic dye market encompasses various types used for diagnostic and therapeutic purposes in eye care. Fluorescein dye is utilized for evaluating corneal health and ocular surface conditions, offering vivid fluorescence under blue light. Rose Bengal stain is employed to detect damage and dryness by highlighting damaged epithelial cells. Trypan blue stain assists in identifying dead or viable cells during surgeries. Other dyes include indocyanine green and methylene blue, serving specific roles in imaging and surgical applications in ophthalmology.</p></p>
