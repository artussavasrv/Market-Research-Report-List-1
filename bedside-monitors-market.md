<p><p>The bedside monitors market is categorized into high-acuity, mid-acuity, and low-acuity monitors based on patient care levels. High-acuity monitors are designed for critically ill patients, providing advanced measurements and continuous monitoring of vital signs. Mid-acuity monitors serve patients needing intermediate monitoring, offering essential parameters without the complexities of high-acuity systems. Low-acuity monitors are used for stable patients in less critical settings, focusing on basic vital signs and ensuring safety in routine care. Each type plays a crucial role in healthcare environments.</p></p>
